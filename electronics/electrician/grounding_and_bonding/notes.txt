*** Bonding Jumper
A bonding jumper is a reliable, low-impedance conductor—typically copper or aluminum—used to ensure electrical continuity between metal parts,
connecting them to the grounding system to prevent shock and fire hazards

*** Effective Ground-Fault Current Path.
An intentionally con-structed low-impedance conductive path designed to carry
fault current from the point of a ground fault on a wiring system
to the electrical supply source.
